Bookstore Sales Have Plummeted Over the Last 10 Years

A look at data shows that the recent low-point of bookstore sales is part of a larger decade-spanning trend.

By Leonardo Lopez Carreno

November 12, 2021

Bookstores have seen a steady decline in sales, US Census Bureau data shows. [Photo via Tony Cenicola/The New York Times]

In 2020, sales from bookstores reached their lowest value in over ten years. This was mostly influenced by the COVID-19 pandemic, which motivated people to buy books online to avoid going outside. However, the low revenue should not be exclusively attributed to these circumstances, as the low sales fall in line with a steady years-long decline.

When asked about what bookstores could do to prevent this, John McKay, Senior Vice President of Communications at the Association of American Publishers, said: “I think that it’s not just up to bookstores, but up to book-lovers as well. […] Whenever I go into a new city, I always look for a bookstore to visit. People have to make a choice to support their local bookstores.”
